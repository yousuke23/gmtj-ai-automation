# Global Music Tour — Guest day sheet (Izu-san area, v0.1, EN)

This document sets **expectations and pacing ideas** for English-speaking guests. It is **not** a substitute for confirmed schedules, prices, or rules from official venues. Staff will share operation-specific details separately.

## What this day is about

- A **music-and-place** experience in the Atami / Izu-san / broader Izu-Hakone region: walking, local culture, optional wellness or voice elements as offered on your booked itinerary.
- Respectful visits to shrines and public spaces; follow **on-site signage** and staff guidance at all times.

## Before you arrive

- **Footwear**: sturdy shoes if your confirmed route includes long stone steps or uneven paths. Open-toe shoes may be unsuitable; follow staff advice for your cohort.
- **Hydration and pacing**: spread effort across the day; take breaks when needed.
- **Weather**: carry a compact layer; trails and viewpoints can be windier or cooler than the coast.
- **Packing**: see [`checklist-packing-izu-san.md`](./checklist-packing-izu-san.md) for a general pre-tour checklist (not a substitute for your booking memo).

## Sample flow (placeholders — confirm on your booking memo)

| Block | Guest experience (high level) |
|-------|--------------------------------|
| Morning | Meet guide/staff; safety brief; shrine or cultural visit if included |
| Midday | Local movement; meal window (dietary restrictions: tell staff in advance) |
| Afternoon | Music / workshop / sightseeing per confirmed program |
| Evening | Rest, onsen, or social time **only as offered** on your booking |

Times, admission rules, and photography policies **vary by site and date**. When in doubt, ask staff on the day or read posted rules before acting.

## Etiquette quick reminders

- Quiet voices near worshippers; no staged “performances” in sacred areas unless explicitly part of an approved program.
- Photos: **only where allowed**; drones and tripods may be restricted—assume “not allowed” unless staff explicitly approve.

## If you feel unwell

- Stop, tell staff, and seek local help. In Japan, **119** is the emergency number for ambulance/fire; **110** is police. Staff will assist with communication where possible.
